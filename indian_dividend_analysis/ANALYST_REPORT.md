1. **50% crash creates entry point:** Stock halved from 52W high while fundamentals improved.
2. **SaaS-like margins in defence:** 37.6% EBITDA margin is exceptional for a hardware company -- indicates strong pricing power.
3. **Arms manufacturing licence:** Recently secured licence to manufacture cannons -- entirely new revenue stream.
4. **Anti-drone dominance:** Won Rs 332 Cr MoD anti-drone order. India's anti-drone needs are growing exponentially.
5. **Fortress balance sheet:** Rs 832 Cr net cash, zero debt. Can self-fund growth without dilution.
6. **Management target: Rs 4,000 Cr revenue** (5.7x current run rate of ~Rs 700 Cr) within 2-3 years.
7. **Export potential:** Defence exports from India growing; Zen's simulation/training products have global applicability.

**4x Math:**
- If FY29 revenue reaches Rs 2,500-3,000 Cr at 30% net margin = Rs 750-900 Cr PAT
- At 35x PE (de-rated from current 48x but still premium for defence): Market cap = Rs 26,250-31,500 Cr
- From current Rs 15,000 Cr = **1.75-2.1x on conservative case**
- If management hits Rs 4,000 Cr target at 30% margin = Rs 1,200 Cr PAT at 40x PE = Rs 48,000 Cr = **3.2x**
- Full 4x requires near-flawless execution + sustained premium valuation

**Key Risks:** High PE (48x), lumpy government order flows, single-client concentration (Indian MoD), execution risk scaling from Rs 700 Cr to Rs 4,000 Cr.

**Conviction Level: 6/10** (higher risk, higher reward -- the "swing for the fences" pick)
